Food Insecurity

Food Insecurity effects a significant amount of the population. Around 15% of all households in the US are food insecure. Of this 15%, 10% have low food security and 5% have very low food security. In certain minority groups, the percent of insecurity is higher. Around 13% of households with children are food insecure. Food Waste

In the United States, food waste is estimated at between 30-40 percent of the food supply. This estimate, of food loss at the retail and consumer levels, corresponded to approximately 133 billion pounds and $161 billion worth of food in 2010.

Not only is this food being wasted but it means that good food that could be given to people who need it end up in landfills, and a countless amount of labor, water and energy is being used to dispose of this discarded food.


Since the Corona pandemic, food waste has increased significantly. Due to the closure of restaurants, hotels and schools huge sources of demand for fresh food has disappeared, leaving farmers with millions of pounds of excess they can not currently sell off. Although this rise in waste is starting to decline for some food, and some farmers have been able to donate to food banks, many farmers are still struggling to find new buyers.
